## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the framework for data governance across the organization. It defines roles, responsibilities, and standards for managing data as a strategic asset while ensuring compliance with applicable regulations.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All employees, contractors, and third parties who access organizational data
- All data assets regardless of format (structured, unstructured, digital, physical)
- All systems, applications, and processes that create, store, process, or transmit data

## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 Data Ownership

- All data must have a designated Data Owner from the business unit that creates or is primarily responsible for the data
- Data Owners are accountable for data quality, access controls, and compliance
- Data Owners must be at the Director level or above

### 3.2 Data Quality

- Data must meet defined quality standards for accuracy, completeness, consistency, and timeliness
- Data quality metrics must be established, measured, and reported quarterly
- Data quality issues must be logged, prioritized, and remediated within defined SLAs:
  - Critical: 24 hours
  - High: 72 hours
  - Medium: 2 weeks
  - Low: Next quarterly review

### 3.3 Data Classification

All data must be classified according to the following scheme:

| Classification | Description | Handling Requirements |
|---------------|-------------|----------------------|
| Public | Information approved for public release | No restrictions |
| Internal | General business information | Access limited to employees |
| Confidential | Sensitive business information | Need-to-know basis, encryption required |
| Restricted | Highly sensitive data (PII, financial) | Strict access controls, encryption at rest and in transit |

### 3.4 Data Access

- Access to data must follow the principle of least privilege
- All access requests must be approved by the Data Owner
- Access must be reviewed quarterly and revoked when no longer needed
- Privileged access requires additional approval from Information Security

### 3.5 Data Retention

- Data must be retained according to the Data Retention Schedule
- Retention periods must comply with legal and regulatory requirements
- Data must be securely disposed of when retention periods expire

### 3.6 Data Privacy

- Personal data processing must comply with GDPR, CCPA, and other applicable privacy regulations
- Privacy Impact Assessments are required for new processing activities involving personal data
- Data Subject requests must be handled within regulatory timeframes

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities

### 4.1 Chief Data Officer (CDO)
- Overall accountability for data governance program
- Approves data policies and standards
- Reports to executive leadership on data governance metrics

### 4.2 Data Governance Council
- Cross-functional body providing oversight and direction
- Meets monthly to review data governance issues and initiatives
- Approves data standards and resolves data ownership disputes

### 4.3 Data Owners
- Business accountability for specific data domains
- Approve access requests and define data quality requirements
- Ensure compliance with policies for their data

### 4.4 Data Stewards
- Day-to-day management of data within their domain
- Monitor data quality and coordinate remediation
- Maintain data documentation and metadata

### 4.5 Data Custodians (IT)
- Technical management of data infrastructure
- Implement security controls and access management
- Support data backup, recovery, and archival

## 5. Compliance

### 5.1 Regulatory Requirements

This policy supports compliance with:
- General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)
- California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)
- Sarbanes-Oxley Act (SOX)
- Basel III/IV requirements
- Industry-specific regulations as applicable

### 5.2 Monitoring and Enforcement

- Compliance with this policy will be monitored through regular audits
- Violations must be reported to the Data Governance Council
- Non-compliance may result in disciplinary action
