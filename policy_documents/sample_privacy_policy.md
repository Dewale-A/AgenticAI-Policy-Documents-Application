**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026

**Classification:** Internal

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the framework for protecting personal data and ensuring compliance with applicable privacy regulations including GDPR, CCPA, and other regional privacy laws.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All employees, contractors, and third parties
- All personal data processed by the organization
- All systems and processes that handle personal data
- All geographic regions where we operate

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Personal Data | Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person |
| Data Subject | The individual whose personal data is being processed |
| Processing | Any operation performed on personal data |
| Controller | Entity that determines purposes and means of processing |
| Processor | Entity that processes data on behalf of controller |

## 4. Data Protection Principles

### 4.1 Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency
- All data processing must have a lawful basis
- Data subjects must be informed about processing activities
- Privacy notices must be clear, concise, and accessible

### 4.2 Purpose Limitation
- Data collected for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes
- No processing incompatible with original purpose without consent

### 4.3 Data Minimization
- Only collect data that is adequate, relevant, and necessary
- Regular review of data holdings to remove unnecessary data

### 4.4 Accuracy
- Personal data must be accurate and kept up to date
- Procedures for data subjects to correct inaccurate data

### 4.5 Storage Limitation
- Data retained only as long as necessary for stated purposes
- Defined retention periods for all data categories
- Secure deletion when retention period expires

### 4.6 Integrity and Confidentiality
- Appropriate security measures to protect personal data
- Protection against unauthorized access, loss, or destruction

## 5. Data Subject Rights

The organization shall facilitate the following rights:

| Right | Response Time | Process Owner |
|-------|---------------|---------------|
| Access | 30 days | Privacy Team |
| Rectification | 30 days | Data Stewards |
| Erasure | 30 days | Privacy Team |
| Restriction | 72 hours | Privacy Team |
| Portability | 30 days | IT Operations |
| Objection | 72 hours | Privacy Team |

## 6. Consent Management

### 6.1 Requirements
- Consent must be freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous
- Clear affirmative action required (no pre-ticked boxes)
- Easy withdrawal mechanism must be provided

### 6.2 Records
- All consent records must be maintained
- Include: who consented, when, how, what they were told

## 7. Data Breach Response

### 7.1 Classification
| Severity | Definition | Notification |
|----------|------------|--------------|
| Critical | Large-scale breach of sensitive data | Regulator within 72 hours |
| High | Breach affecting >1000 individuals | Regulator within 72 hours |
| Medium | Limited breach with low risk | Internal review required |
| Low | Near-miss or contained incident | Log and monitor |

### 7.2 Response Procedure
1. Contain and assess the breach
2. Notify Privacy Officer immediately
3. Document all facts and actions taken
4. Determine notification requirements
5. Implement remediation measures
6. Conduct post-incident review

## 8. Third-Party Management

### 8.1 Due Diligence
- Privacy impact assessment for all new vendors
- Review of vendor security certifications
- Contractual data protection clauses required

### 8.2 Data Processing Agreements
All third-party processors must have agreements covering:
- Nature and purpose of processing
- Types of personal data
- Security requirements
- Sub-processor restrictions
- Audit rights

## 9. International Transfers

### 9.1 Transfer Mechanisms
- Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
- Binding Corporate Rules
- Adequacy decisions
- Explicit consent (limited circumstances)

### 9.2 Transfer Impact Assessments
Required for all transfers to countries without adequacy decisions.

## 12. Monitoring and Audit

- Quarterly privacy compliance reviews
- Annual external privacy audit
- Continuous monitoring of data processing activities
- Regular testing of breach response procedures

## 13. Policy Violations

Violations may result in:
- Disciplinary action up to termination
- Regulatory fines and penalties
- Legal liability
- Reputational damage
