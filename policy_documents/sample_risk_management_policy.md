## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the Enterprise Risk Management (ERM) framework for identifying, assessing, managing, and monitoring risks across the organization to protect stakeholder value and ensure achievement of strategic objectives.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All business units, functions, and subsidiaries
- All types of risk: strategic, operational, financial, compliance, and reputational
- All employees with risk management responsibilities

## 3. Risk Management Principles

### 3.1 Risk Culture
- Risk management is everyone's responsibility
- Open communication about risks is encouraged and expected
- Risk-aware decision making is embedded in all business processes

### 3.2 Risk Appetite
- The Board of Directors sets overall risk appetite annually
- Business units operate within defined risk tolerance levels
- Risk limits are monitored continuously with escalation procedures

### 3.3 Three Lines of Defense

| Line | Function | Responsibilities |
|------|----------|------------------|
| First | Business Operations | Own and manage risks, implement controls |
| Second | Risk & Compliance | Oversight, frameworks, policies, monitoring |
| Third | Internal Audit | Independent assurance and validation |

## 4. Risk Management Process

### 4.1 Risk Identification
- Risks must be identified through multiple channels:
  - Business planning processes
  - Incident and near-miss reporting
  - Control assessments
  - External monitoring
- All identified risks must be logged in the Risk Register

### 4.2 Risk Assessment

Risks are assessed using a 5x5 matrix:

**Likelihood Scale:**
1. Rare (< 5% probability)
2. Unlikely (5-25%)
3. Possible (25-50%)
4. Likely (50-75%)
5. Almost Certain (> 75%)

**Impact Scale:**
1. Negligible (< $100K)
2. Minor ($100K - $1M)
3. Moderate ($1M - $10M)
4. Major ($10M - $50M)
5. Severe (> $50M)

### 4.3 Risk Response

Acceptable risk responses:
- **Accept:** Risk within appetite, no action required
- **Mitigate:** Implement controls to reduce likelihood/impact
- **Transfer:** Insurance, contracts, outsourcing
- **Avoid:** Cease activity creating the risk

### 4.4 Risk Monitoring
- Key Risk Indicators (KRIs) tracked monthly
- Risk Register reviewed quarterly
- Emerging risks assessed continuously
- Board risk report provided quarterly

## 5. Operational Risk

### 5.1 Definition
Operational risk is the risk of loss resulting from inadequate or failed:
- Internal processes
- People and culture
- Systems and technology
- External events

### 5.2 Key Operational Risk Categories
- Process execution errors
- Fraud (internal and external)
- Technology failures
- Business continuity events
- Third-party/vendor risks
- Regulatory compliance failures

### 5.3 Operational Risk Controls
- Segregation of duties
- System access controls
- Transaction limits and approvals
- Reconciliation processes
- Business continuity plans

## 6. Roles and Responsibilities

### 6.1 Board of Directors
- Approve risk appetite statement
- Oversee enterprise risk profile
- Review significant risk exposures

### 6.2 Chief Risk Officer
- Lead ERM program
- Report to Board on risk matters
- Escalate material risks

### 6.3 Risk Committee
- Oversee risk management activities
- Review and approve risk policies
- Monitor risk profile against appetite

### 6.4 Business Unit Managers
- Identify and assess risks in their area
- Implement and maintain controls
- Report risk events and near-misses

## 7. Compliance Requirements

This policy supports compliance with:
- Basel III/IV Operational Risk Framework
- SOX Section 404
- OCC Heightened Standards
- Federal Reserve SR 11-7 (Model Risk)
